My Role & Responsibilities

UX Design Intern:
For my UX design internship, I had the incredible opportunity to work with the Corporate Applications team at Blizzard to enhance the capabilities of their conference room displays and improve the effeciency around locating and booking rooms. I worked closely with the design manager, PM, and fellow engineers on the team to iterate and deliver a conference room finder/discovery interface. Problem


My Design Process


Problem Statement

There exists no option for employees to find and book a conference through the conference room displays. Conference room discovery and booking is only supported via the internal web application, which is not always optimal.

The only existing way to reserve a conference room is through the its display or the internal web application.

Solution

A new conference room discovery and booking feature, on the displays, allowing employees to quickly locate and book an available conference room to reduce the total time spent, across employees, searching for available rooms.

User Research

What is a CRD?

CRDs (conference room displays) are iPads, located at each conference room, powering an internal application which allow employees to book cpnference rooms and view the room's schedule.

Assumptions

These are some assumptions that were made prior to user research:

  • The only way to book a conference room is through the CRD and web application.
  • Finding a conference room that meets a set of criteria is time consuming and difficult.
  • There are few accessible building maps on campus.

User Interviews

User interviews were conducted with employees across different levels of experience and roles to inquire about their workflow, experiences, and pain points with utilizing the conference room displays.

Research Conclusions

  • Conference rooms are often booked while left unused.
  • The internal web application is the only existing option to locate and book conference rooms.
  • A significant amount of time is spent navigating through unfamiliar buildings while searching for conference rooms.

Screen 5: Booking a different conference room experience

User Story: As a user, I want to ensure that I am on the correct CRD's page so that I can reserve the correct conference room.

Overview: A component at the top of the application screen flags that a user is on a CRD page for a different confernce room.


Screen 6: Confirmation modal for inactivity

User Story: As a user, I want a modal confirmation for inactivity before being redirected back to the home CRD page so that I have the option to continue reserving a conference room.

Overview: This is the end of the user flow. After one minute, a confirmation modal will ask the user if more time is required to reserve the room. This ensures that users who are actively booking a room doesn't get redirected to the CRD's home page. The screen will automatically redirect to the CRD home page after some period of inactivity.

Person Finder

User Story: As an employee, I want the ability to view another employee's desk location so that I can quickly locate and access them.

Overview: This feature allows users to search and locate the desk locations of all employees.

Problem: The internal web application is the only existing option to locate an employee's desk location.

Result:

Conference Room Check-in

User Story: As a user, I want the ability to check-in to a conference room from the display so that others are informed that the room is currently occupied.

Overview:The check-in feature prevents a conference room from remaining booked and left unused. If the attendees do not check-in with five minutes after the start time, the room will automatically reject the meeting and become available.

Problem:The user interviews consistently demonstrated that employees often encounter booked but unused conference rooms. The employees will then go on to message the attendees to confirm its usage.

Outcome:

Internship Reflection

After finishing my UX design internship at Blizzard Entertainment, I actively reflected on my internship experience in attempt to learn and improve. Here are some of my learnings:

Be confident in your own judgement

  • I realized early on that it is important to exercise my own judgement and communicate my opinions early in the process while taking feedback into account.

Be proactive

  • Being proactive allowed me to lead and conduct user research early, critique the team's user flows/designs, and design additional features outside of my project. It also provided me the opportunity to identify and improve other experiences and inefficiencies around booking conference rooms.

Don't be afraid to ask questions and voice your opinions

  • As an intern, it can be frightening to voice your opinions during meetings with your superiors. Most of the hesitation comes from the self-inflicted pressure to deliver intelligent and organized thoughts. However, my mentor had told me that "there is no wrong answer and it only gets easier with practice." Realizing that it only gets easier through practice and iterations has helped me break through this barrier early on.
