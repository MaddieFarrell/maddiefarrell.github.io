My Role & Responsibilities

UX Design Intern:
Summer 2020, I had the incredible opportunity to work with the Corporate Applications team at Blizzard Entertainment to enhance the capabilities of their conference room displays. Throughout my internship, I worked closely with my manager, pm, and engineers to design my main project, which is a conference room finder/discovery feature. I conducted user research and utilized design tools like Figma to create user flows and prototypes. Towards the end of my internship, I was able to research, pitch, and design my own ideas that will maximize conference room utilization and reduce room search time across employees.

Corporate Applications

The Corporate Applications team is responsible for creating innovative and transformative technologies to enable all of Blizzard to deliver and exceed on making epic experiences that engage and delight users.

My Design Process


Problem Statement

The only way to to reserve another conference room is through a rooms own CRD or Blizzard's web application.

Solution

A room finder feature that will be added to the conference room display, which allows employees to find and book a conference room from any display. Allowing employees to save time in finding and booking conference rooms.

User Research

What is a CRD?

CRD stands for conference room display. They are located in front of all of the conference rooms and allows employees to book and view the rooms schedule.

Assumptions

An assumption is formed out of the given problem and is assumed to be true. These are some of my assumptions that I made before user research:

  • The only way to book a conference room is through the CRD and web application.
  • Finding a conference room that fits an employees specification can be time consuming and stressful.
  • There are few accessible building maps on campus.

User Interviews

User interviews were conducted with Blizzard employees throughout the company with ranging levels of experience and job titles. I was able to learn about their experiences with booking and locating conference rooms and any other pain points related to the CRD.

Research Conclusions

  • Meeting rooms are often booked while left unused.
  • Besides walking around campus, the company directory is the only way to locate another conference room
  • Navigating through unfamiliar Blizzard buildings can be difficult.

Screen 2: Conference room finder - Map view

User Story: As a user I want there to be a away to find other conference rooms so that I can book other rooms more easily.

Overview: The conference room finder will have a map view. It allows users to view meeting rooms around their current location and the ability to filter rooms based on capacity, features, building and floor number, and currently available rooms.


Screen 3: Conference room finder - list view

User Story: As a user, I want to be able to view all of the conference rooms, that fits my requirements so that I can find and book a room ideal for my meeting.

Overview: Below is the list view on the conference room finder. I includes a list of all of the conference rooms that are in that office location along with the advanced search to narrow down the conference rooms.


Screen 4: Experience when a user taps on a room in map view

User Story: As a user, I want to be able to view all of the conference rooms, that fits my requirements so that I can find and book a room ideal for my meeting.

Overview: When a user finds and taps on a conference room, a modal will pop up showing them the meeting rooms information, including navigation to reserve the room.


Screen 5: Booking a different conference room experience

User Story: As a user, I want to ensure that I am on the correct CRDs landing page so that I can reserve the correct conference room.

Overview: A yellow bar at the top of the screen will appear to inform the user that they are on the CRD landing page of a different meeting room, not the one that they are standing in front of.


Screen 6: Inactivity confirmation page

User Story: As a user, I want an inactivity confirmation page, so that I don't lose my form data on the CRD, and can continue reserving my conference room

Overview: This is the end of the user flow. When the 2-minute time runs out, a modal will pop up asking the user if they need more time to reserve the room. This ensures that users who need more time won't lose any of their form data and they can have more time to reserve. After a short amount of inactivity, the screen will automatically switch back to the CRD landing page screen.

Person Finder

User Story: As an employee, I want to be able to find other employees, so that I can find them to talk to them or if I need to ask them questions.

Overview: This feature allows users to find the desk location of other employees through the company.

Problem: Word of mouth or the company directory is the only way to locate another employee's desk. Adding a person finder will increase employee productivity.

Future additions: One feature to add in the future is giving the users the ability to ping another employee from a CRD. This lets the pinged person know that someone is looking for them and which CRD they can be found at.

Meeting room check-in

User Story: As a user, I want a check-in feature on the CRD, so that if a room is booked for the current time and no one is in the room or checked-in, the room can open up and lets other employees book the room.

Overview: Check-in feature removes the possibility of booked meeting rooms left unused.

Problem: A pain point brought up more than once in user interviews is that employees often find themselves walking up to an unused conference room but the CRD claims it's booked.

Internship Reflection

Since the end of my UX design internship at Blizzard Entertainment, I am able to reflect on my experience and learn from it. Here are some of my reflections:

Be confident in your judgement

  • Early on, I realized that although it is important to take feedback into account for my designs, I should make sure that I exercise my own judgment as well.

Be proactive and take charge

  • Throughout my intership, I learned how to be more proactive when it came to taking charge of the user research, user flows, designs, and any additional features.

Don't be afraid to speak up or ask questions

  • As an intern, it can be frightening to speak up in meetings. This is because you're in a new place with new people, most of whom are more senior. A lot of the hesitation around speaking up comes from the self-inflicted pressure to deliver smart and fully formed thoughts. Early on, I learned that the more you speak up, the more comfortable you will get. One thing that I was told by my mentor is that "there is no wrong answer and even the senior designers mess up all of the time".
