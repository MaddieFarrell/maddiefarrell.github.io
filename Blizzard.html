My Role & Responsibilities

UX Design Intern:
At Blizzard, I worked as a UX design intern on the Corporate Applications team. My main project was designing a conference room finder feature for the conference room displays. After I completed my project, I was able to propose and design features for the CRD(conference room display) that I believed would benefit the company. My Design Process


Problem Statement

When an employee wants to reserve a conference room but the conference room is booked, the only way they can reserve another conference room is by walking to another room or by booking from their computer.

Solution

The solution is to design a feature that will be added to the conference room display, which allows employees to find and book a conference room from any display. It will also allow employees to save time in finding and booking conference rooms, as well as finding meeting rooms that fit their specifications.

User Research

What is a CRD?

CRD stands for conference room display. CRD's are located in front of all of the conference rooms. They allow employees to view the room's schedule along with the ability to reserve the room.

Assumptions

An assumption is formed out of the given problem and is assumed to be true. These are some of my assumptions that I made before user research:

  • Conference rooms often get double booked.
  • There is currently no other way to book a conference room besides walking up to that conference room display or by booking the meeting room on a computer.
  • Finding a conference room that fits an employees specification can be time consuming and stressful.

User Interviews

User interviews are a core user experience method. I was able to conduct user interviews with a number of Blizzard employees across the company with different years of experience and job titles. Through these user interviews, I was able to talk with them and learn about their experiences with booking and locating conference rooms and any other pain points that they have encountered.

Research Conclusions

  • There are a lot of times where a meeting room booked, but no one is in the room.
  • The only way to view and locate other conference rooms is through the company directory.
  • Navigating through other Blizzard buildings that you are not familiar with can be difficult.

Person Finder

User Story: As an employee, I want to be able to find other employees, so that I can find them to talk to them or if I need to ask them questions.

Overview: This feature allows users to find the desk location of other employees through the CRD

Problem: The only way to locate another employee's desk is by going to the Blizzards directory or by asking someone directly. Adding a person finder to the CRD will help employee productivity and save a lot of time and effort.

Future additions: One cool feature to add to the person finder is giving users the ability to ping another employee from a CRD. This lets the person who is being searched for know that someone is looking for them and which CRD they can be found at.

Outcome: Users will be able to find other employees through a list view. The page enables users to search and sort employees by title, email, department, and location. Once a user finds the employee they want to locate, they can choose to tap the locate button that brings them to a map that shows where that person's desk is.

Meeting room check-in

User Story: As a user, I want a check-in feature on the CRD, so that if a room is booked for the current time and no one is in the room or checked-in, the room can open up and lets other employees book the room.

Overview: Check-in removes ghost meetings.

Problem: While conducting user interviews earlier on in my internship, there was one pain point that was brought up more than once. The pain point is that employees often find themselves walking up to an empty conference room but the CRD shows that it is currently booked.

Future additions: One feature that I would add if I had more time would be a "book now" feature. This feature will allow users to quickly book a room in small intervals, starting from 15 min and up to 1 hour.

Outcome: Adding a check-in feature removes the possibility of ghost meetings. This means that if nobody shows up to the meeting after a set amount of time and doesn't check-in, then the room will unbook itself. When a room unbooks itself, a notification will be sent out to all employees who were supposed to be at that meeting.

Internship Reflection

Since the end of my UX design internship at Blizzard Entertainment, I am able to reflect on my experience and learn from it. Here are some of my reflections:

Be confident in your judgement

  • Early on, I realized that although it is important to take feedback into account for my designs, I should make sure that I exercise my own judgment as well.

Be proactive and take charge

  • During the internship, I learned how to be more proactive. This is because when I began my project, I was in charge of all of the user research, designs, and the features that I want added. Also, I was able to complete my project early, which game me the opportunity to pitch my ideas for the CRD and then take immediate action by designing it out.

Don't be afraid to speak up or ask questions

  • As an intern, it can be frightening to speak up in meetings. This is because you're in a new place with new people, most of whom are more senior. A lot of the hesitation around speaking up comes from the self-inflicted pressure to deliver smart and fully formed thoughts. Early on, I learned that the more you speak up, the more comfortable you will get. One thing that I was told by my mentor is that "there is no wrong answer and even the senior designers mess up all of the time".
