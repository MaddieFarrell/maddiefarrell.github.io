Evolve

Evolve is a fitness app that uses artificial intellegince to create workouts that are personalized for our users to ensure that they will meet their goals. To create users custom-fit workout, Evolve uses AU to understand our users strength-training ability studies their past workouts and adapts to their workout settings.

My Role:

Product Designer:

I designed the branding and logo for Evolve and the mobile application. Besides working on the designs and information architecure, I also performed user research to understand more about our target users.

User Research

I conducted user research to gain a better understanding of our user’s needs, behaviors, experience, and motivations involving their fitness journey.

Research Goals

  1. To better understand our target users.
  2. To discover users' pain points and frustrations around their fitness journey.
  3. To learn more about their process with figuring out what workouts they should do to meet their goals
  4. To create, validate, and iterate on potential design solutions.

Methods

  • Market Research
  • Survey

Market Research

I did some market research so that I can unnderstand uncover the high-level information regarding the fitness industry. Market research is also helpful with forming our marketing, sales and brand strategy. It also makes sure that our product speaks directly to what the market wants, needs, or doesn’t yet know it wants and needs. Below is some of my market research results.

  1. About one in three Americansreport at some point having worn a fitness tracker such as a Fitbit or smartwatch or having tracked their health statistics on a phone or tablet app.
  2. High-income households, young adults and women among top users of fitness apps
  3. Revenues for health and fitness mobile apps are also thriving, reaching $0.51 billion in 2015 and $2.1 billion in 2017. Polaris Market Research forecasts that the global health app market size will reach $14.7 billion by 2026. Other sources are slightly more conservative, putting the figure at $10.9 billion.

User Interviews

I interviewed a series of people who have used other fitness apps, want to get into fitness but doesn't know where to begin, and users who are already into fitness.

These were a few questions that I aimed to clarify through the user interviews:

  1. What are the hardest parts when starting your workout journey?
  2. How do users find the right workouts?
  3. What would they like to improve on pertaining to their workouts?
  4. Have they ever used any other fitness apps? If yes, why and what do they like and dislike? If no, why not?

Research Conclusions

After completing all of the user interviews, I took all of the information that I gathered and sorted it based on commonalities. Below are some of the most common answers that I gathered that also answered my questions that I wanted to clarify.

  • For most people, the hardest part of starting a workout is motivation. The most common phrase I heard was "I'm lazy"
  • They don't know what workouts they should do to meet their goals, so they end up googling random workouts.
  • Users want to be held accountable to complete their workouts.
  • Around 50% percent of the people I intervied have tried out a fitness app, most of those users were women.

High-Fidelity Mockups

After creating my sketches and testing their usability, I jumped into prototyping my final design solution.

Splash Screen

Splash screens are very important for mobile UX design. It is a short introductory screen that sets the tone for the rest of the user experience and ensures each users’ early experience is a positive one.

User Sign-Up Flow

The first step a new user has to undergo is signing up. When signing up, users will enter their phone number and password. After that is complete they will get a sms verifcation code sent to them. I added a confirmation code so we can decrease the amount of spammers and to also protect our users information. After entering in the verification code, they will have successfuly signed up and will be navigated to the questionaire.

Onboarding

These screens are added to introduce our users to Evolve. Having a few onboarding screens eases our users into the app and informs them about how Evolve will help them.

Questionaire Screens

To make sure that Evolve provides the best workout program for our users, we have to know some information about them before they begin. This information includes: name, age, weight, height, their fitness experience, and their fitness goals. We use this information to caluclate the most optimum sets, reps, and weight.

Home Screen

On the home page, we have made all the features of our app accessible, such as, that days workout, bodyweight tracking, calorie counter, workout history, and the settigs. Users will have the option to change weather they are working out at a gym or at home and the length of the workout they want to do.

Bodyweight Tracker

Workout Screen

Alternate Exercises

When having users test out Evolve, one pain point came up quite a few times which is wanting to change an exercise. There is a couple reasons for this such as the exercise is to hard for them, they are not able to do it due to injury, or they simply don't like that exercise. Based on this feedback,I designed an feature that allows users to chose a related exercise to replatce the one that they don't want to do.

Calorie Tracker

The calorie tracker allows users to view on average how much calories they burn during their workouts. They have the option to view just one workout session up to all of the workout sessions that they have completed on Evolve.
