Evolve

Evolve is a fitness app that uses artificial intellegince to generate personalized workouts for our users. Our artificial intelligence is based on thousands of workout programs designed by the worldâ€™s top celebrity and professional athlete trainers to optimize your workout routine based on your training goals, experience, and performance. To create users custom-fit workout, Evolve uses AI to understand our users strength-training ability, studies their past workouts, and adapts to their workout settings.


My Role:

Product Designer:

Led the designs for the mobile application, website, company branding and logo. In addition, user research was conducted to learn more about the target users and create initial user flows.

User Research

User research was conducted to better understand the users' needs, experience, and motivations involving their relationship and journey with fitness.


Research Goals

  • To better understand our target users and their relationship with fitness.
  • To discover users' pain points and frustrations around their fitness journey.
  • To learn more about the users' process on workout discovery to obtain their goals.
  • To create, test, validate, and iterate on the minimum viable product.

Methods

  • Market Research
  • Surveys
  • User Interviews

Market Research

Market research was completed to better understand the fitness industry and to help design the core marketing and brand strategies. The following are some of my market research results. Source

  • About one in three Americans report at some point having worn a fitness tracker such as a Fitbit or smartwatch or having tracked their health statistics on a phone or tablet app.
  • High-income households, young adults and women among top users of fitness apps
  • Revenues for health and fitness mobile apps are also thriving, reaching $510 million in 2015 and $2.1 billion in 2017. Polaris Market Research forecasts that the global health app market size will reach $14.7 billion by 2026. Other sources are slightly more conservative, putting the figure at $10.9 billion.

Market Opportunity (Why now?)

  • People are becoming more conscious about their health, lifestyle, and fitness.
  • Mobile and internet usage is at its peak with the recent tech boom and covid-19.
  • The fitness social media influencer market is growing at an alarming rate.

User Interviews

User interviews were conducted in order to get better insights on the users' core problem. Here are some of the questions asked:

  • What is your relationship with fitness?
  • What is the hardest parts about working out?
  • When was the last time you encountered this problem?
  • Have you tried to solve this problem?
  • What don't you like about existing solutions you've tried?
  • How do you discover and choose workouts to perform?
  • What would you like to improve about your workouts?
  • Have they ever used any other fitness apps? If yes, why and what do they like and dislike? If no, why not?




Research Insights

After completing the user interviews, all of the information gathered was sorted by its commonalities. Here are some of the most common answers that were collected.

  • Over 85% of interviewees said the most difficult part of working out is due to the lack of motivation.
  • Approximately 30% of interviewees don't know what workouts to perform to acheive their goals.
  • Users generally want to be held accountable to complete their workouts.
  • Approximately 50% percent of interviewees has tried using a fitness app.
  • Users want to see data on progression in the form of strength gain or weight loss.

Target Users

As a part of the design process, the users' needs and insight were defined based on market research and user research. Here are the users that we are targeting:

  • People getting into fitness who want to gain muscle or lose weight
  • People who value personalization vs. standard plans
  • Beginners who need the push to follow a routine
  • People with time constraints and a budget

High-Fidelity Mockups

After creating the sketches and testing its usability, I started prototyping my final design solution.


Splash Screen

The splash screen provides a short introduction to our app and sets the tone for the rest of the user experience.

User Sign-Up Flow

Users will provide their phone number and password to sign-up. SMS code verification is implemented to verify the identity of the user. Post verification, the user is redirected to the onboarding flow.


Onboarding Flow

The onboarding screens inform our users on what to expect and how to use the application.


Questionaire Flow

To provide the best experience and exercise plan for our users, the questionnaire flow collects information about our users. The information collected includes: name, age, weight, height, fitness experience, and fitness goals. This information is used to calculate the optimal combination of exercises, sets, reps, and weight.


Main Screen

Evolve prepares new personalized workout routines each day for our users. Our algorithm uses our users past workout performance, recovery-period, and fitness goals to generate new workout routines with suggested progression and difficulty optimized to help our users achieve their fitness goals. As they continue to improve, we will adapt and increase the difficulty in their next workout.


Workout Screen

The workout screen represents the active workout session. The workout sessions are designed using proven training methods adopted by celebrity and professional athlete trainers and includes proven workout methods such as progressive overload, DUP, and high volume training.


Bodyweight Tracker

Based our user interviews, a high representation of our users' focus is weight loss. The bodyweight tracker helps our users track the metric that is most important to them.


Alternate Exercises

We discovered that users wanted the ability to change an exercise during our user testing phase. The core reasons include: difficulty of exercise, available equipment, and preferences. The alternate exercise feature allows users to replace an existing exercise with a related one.


Workout History

Users have demonstrated that they care deeply about seeing and tracking progression. The workout history feature allows users to check their workout history and streak to keep our users motivated. Streaks has driven competition and motivation to workout across our users in hopes of keeping the streak.


Calorie Tracker

The calorie tracker allows users to view the amount of calories burned during workouts. We discovered that visually seeing a concrete number drives motivation in starting a workout session.
